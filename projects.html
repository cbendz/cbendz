GameChanger: Promoting Health and Wellbeing with Games



GameChanger is an innovative project to create mobile and Kinect exergames to promote health and wellbeing. Our project is particularly concerned with improving health for young adults. Over a third of young adults are overweight and only 37% exercise regularly. Rates of physical activity decline with age; the steepest decline occurs during young adulthood. We clearly need to find ways to make even simple physical activity rewarding, especially for our younger generations. Technology is more likely to help young adults achieve new positive health behaviors. The primary objective of this research is to create prototypes for a new generation of social exergames that interweave physical activity as a core game mechanic with social elements such as competition and collaboration to motivate long-term adherence to physical activity. The net result of this work will be a representative suite of social exergames that have been implemented, iteratively refined, and empirically evaluated. GameChanger prototypes will be designed as platforms for experimentation to determine which combinations of social and physical elements have the greatest impact on the physical activity.

MAD Science



In the MAD Science program, middle school students become philanthropists tasked with creating a participatory sensing application. Using a mobile phone as a sensor, the students collect data samples, analyze the results, and present the results to their community. Their mission: identify and address a civic problem in their community.
